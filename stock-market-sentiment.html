Stock Market Sentiment Guide

Stock market sentiment reflects public narrative around equity markets, indexes, futures, earnings, interest rates, volatility, and macro expectations.

What drives stock market sentiment?

  • Earnings expectations and corporate guidance
  • Interest rates and Federal Reserve policy
  • Inflation and jobs data
  • Risk appetite and volatility
  • Geopolitics and tariffs
  • Sector rotation and mega-cap leadership

Indexes covered

PublicSentimentDash tracks public narrative around major equity indexes and futures including the S&P 500, Nasdaq, Dow, Russell 2000, VIX, DAX, FTSE 100, Nikkei 225, Hang Seng, Euro Stoxx 50, and CAC 40.

How to use stock market sentiment

Public sentiment can help identify whether the market narrative is focused on fear, optimism, rate pressure, earnings strength, recession risk, volatility, or rotation.

It should be compared with chart trend, earnings reality, liquidity, macro data, and valuation conditions.
