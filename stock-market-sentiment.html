Stock Market Guide

Stock Market Sentiment

Stock market sentiment is the public mood around equities, indexes, futures, earnings, volatility, rates, economic data, and risk appetite.

Public Sentiment Dash helps organize equity-related headlines so users can quickly see whether the public narrative is leaning bullish, bearish, mixed, or neutral.

Index Sentiment

Tracks public narrative around the S&P 500, Nasdaq, Dow, Russell 2000, VIX, and major global indexes.

Risk Appetite

Highlights whether headlines point toward confidence, fear, volatility, recession concern, earnings pressure, or market optimism.

Macro Drivers

Connects stock sentiment with rates, inflation, jobs, GDP, central banks, geopolitical risk, and earnings expectations.

What Moves Stock Market Sentiment?

Stock sentiment often changes when traders react to earnings, interest rates, inflation, employment data, Federal Reserve comments, bond yields, volatility, credit risk, and major company headlines.

A bullish headline environment does not guarantee prices will rise. It simply shows that the public narrative is leaning positive. Users should compare sentiment with technical and fundamental analysis.
