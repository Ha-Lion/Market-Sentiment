Commodity Sentiment Guide

Commodity sentiment reflects public narrative around raw materials such as gold, silver, copper, crude oil, and natural gas.

What drives commodity sentiment?

  • Supply and demand expectations
  • Inflation and interest-rate expectations
  • Geopolitical risk
  • Inventory data
  • Weather and seasonal demand
  • Industrial growth or recession expectations

Gold and silver sentiment

Gold and silver sentiment often reacts to inflation, real yields, U.S. dollar strength, central-bank policy, geopolitical stress, and safe-haven demand.

Oil and natural gas sentiment

Crude oil and natural gas sentiment can shift quickly around supply disruptions, OPEC decisions, inventory reports, weather, demand expectations, and geopolitical headlines.

Copper sentiment

Copper is often watched as an industrial growth signal. Public narrative may focus on China demand, construction activity, manufacturing, supply disruptions, and global growth expectations.

How to use commodity sentiment

Commodity sentiment should be read alongside supply-demand data, macro conditions, chart trend, currency movement, and event risk.
