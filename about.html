About PublicSentimentDash

PublicSentimentDash is a market-awareness tool built to help traders, investors, and market watchers quickly understand where public financial narrative is leaning.

The dashboard focuses mainly on the public sentiment layer, while also showing technical direction and helping users place sentiment within broader fundamental market context.

What markets are really made of

Financial markets move because many forces interact at the same time: economic data, rates, earnings, liquidity, policy, geopolitics, positioning, emotion, momentum, and public narrative.

No single tool explains everything. That is why many serious traders and investors look at three major layers together:

1) Public Sentiment

What the public and financial media are emphasizing right now. This captures tone, narrative, fear, optimism, and attention.

2) Fundamental Analysis

The underlying economic and financial reality: inflation, rates, jobs, GDP, earnings, balance sheets, policy, and supply-demand conditions.

3) Technical Analysis

What price itself is doing: trend, momentum, moving averages, support/resistance, and whether buyers or sellers currently control the chart.

Public Sentiment

Public sentiment reflects how financial headlines and widely discussed market topics are leaning at a given moment.

This can matter because narrative often affects:

  • short-term market mood
  • risk appetite
  • panic or enthusiasm
  • attention toward specific assets
  • the speed at which themes spread

What this dashboard does with sentiment

  • scans financial/public market sources
  • collects relevant market headlines
  • classifies them by market and instrument
  • estimates vote direction such as bullish, bearish, mixed, or neutral
  • shows top themes, market focus, and relevant headlines

Important limitation

Public sentiment is not the same thing as truth, value, or a trade signal. Sometimes narrative leads price, sometimes it lags price, and sometimes it is simply noisy.

Fundamental Analysis

Fundamental analysis looks at the real forces behind an asset or market.

Examples of fundamentals

  • Stocks / Indexes: earnings, revenue growth, margins, guidance, rates, valuations, macro conditions
  • Forex: central bank policy, inflation, jobs, growth, yield differentials, trade balance
  • Bonds: inflation expectations, growth outlook, Treasury issuance, Fed policy, recession risk
  • Commodities: supply, demand, inventories, geopolitics, weather, OPEC decisions, industrial demand
  • Crypto: adoption, regulation, liquidity, market structure, network usage, macro risk appetite

Why fundamentals matter

Over time, fundamentals often provide the deeper “why” behind large and lasting market moves.

Public sentiment may help explain the current mood. Technicals may help show current structure. Fundamentals help explain the broader engine underneath.

Technical Analysis

Technical analysis studies price behavior directly. It helps answer practical questions such as:

  • Is the market trending or ranging?
  • Is momentum improving or weakening?
  • Are buyers or sellers in control?
  • Is the chart structure aligned with the story?

Technical direction on this dashboard

The dashboard calculates daily, weekly, and monthly direction using price-based checks such as:

  • SMA20
  • SMA50
  • close vs SMA20
  • close vs SMA50
  • SMA20 vs SMA50 trend
  • 10-candle slope
  • RSI14

Those inputs are combined into a simple direction label such as Bullish, Bearish, Mixed/Bullish, Mixed/Bearish, or Neutral.

Important limitation

Technical analysis is useful, but it is still an interpretation tool. It does not predict the future with certainty.

How to use all three together

The strongest decision-making usually happens when sentiment, fundamentals, and technicals are considered together.

Example: Strong Alignment

Public sentiment is bullish, fundamentals are supportive, and technical direction is bullish. This is stronger alignment.

Example: Mixed Picture

Public sentiment is bullish, but fundamentals are weak and technicals are rolling over. This is a warning sign.

Example: Early Narrative Shift

Public sentiment starts improving before charts fully turn. That may signal an early theme change, but it still needs confirmation.

Important reminder

This website is designed as a fast market-awareness tool, not as a promise, prediction engine, or financial recommendation system.

Public sentiment can be noisy. Fundamentals can take time to matter. Technicals can fail. Markets can behave irrationally longer than expected.

The best use of this dashboard is as part of a broader decision process.
