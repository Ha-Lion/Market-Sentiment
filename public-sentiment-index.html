Public Sentiment Index for Global Markets

Public Sentiment Dash tracks AI-assisted public market sentiment across stocks, forex, crypto, commodities, bonds, and macro headlines.

The Public Sentiment Index, or PSI, is designed to give a quick reading of how public financial narratives are leaning across major markets.

What the Public Sentiment Index Measures

The Public Sentiment Index organizes public headlines and market narratives into a simple sentiment score. It helps users quickly understand whether the current public discussion appears bullish, bearish, mixed, or neutral.

Fresh Market Headlines

The index prioritizes recent, relevant public headlines from major financial news sources.

Market Narrative

The dashboard watches for bullish, bearish, high-impact, macro, earnings, risk, inflation, and central-bank language.

Cross-Market Coverage

Coverage includes indexes, stocks, forex, crypto, commodities, bonds, and macroeconomic themes.

How to Read PSI

  • 0 to 30: Bearish public sentiment.
  • 31 to 44: Mixed / bearish sentiment.
  • 45 to 55: Mixed or neutral sentiment.
  • 56 to 69: Mixed / bullish sentiment.
  • 70 to 100: Bullish public sentiment.

PSI is not a trading signal. It is a quick public-sentiment context tool.

Markets Covered

Public Sentiment Dash tracks major areas of public financial discussion:

  • Stock market sentiment and index sentiment.
  • S&P 500, Nasdaq, Dow, Russell, VIX, and global indexes.
  • Forex sentiment, including US Dollar / DXY, EUR/USD, GBP/USD, USD/JPY, and other currency pairs.
  • Crypto sentiment, including Bitcoin, Ethereum, Solana, XRP, BNB, Cardano, and Dogecoin.
  • Commodity sentiment, including gold, silver, copper, crude oil, and natural gas.
  • Macro themes including Federal Reserve, inflation, CPI, PPI, jobs, GDP, tariffs, and geopolitical risk.
