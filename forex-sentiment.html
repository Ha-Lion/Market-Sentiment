Forex Sentiment

Forex sentiment is the public mood around currencies, central banks, interest rates, inflation, growth data, political risk, and safe-haven demand.

Public Sentiment Dash helps organize currency-related headlines so users can quickly see whether the public narrative is leaning bullish, bearish, mixed, or neutral.

Dollar Strength

Tracks public narrative around DXY, Federal Reserve expectations, Treasury yields, inflation, and U.S. macro data.

Major Pairs

Covers EUR/USD, GBP/USD, USD/JPY, USD/CAD, AUD/USD, NZD/USD, and other major currency themes.

Macro Drivers

Highlights headlines around rates, central banks, inflation, jobs, GDP, geopolitical risk, and risk appetite.

What Moves Forex Sentiment?

Forex sentiment often reacts to interest-rate expectations, inflation reports, jobs data, central bank speeches, risk appetite, bond yields, political events, and safe-haven flows.
