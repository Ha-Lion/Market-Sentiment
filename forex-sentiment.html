Forex Sentiment Guide

Forex sentiment reflects how the public market narrative is leaning around currencies, central banks, inflation, yields, risk appetite, and global macro conditions.

What drives forex sentiment?

  • Central bank policy and interest-rate expectations
  • Inflation reports such as CPI and PPI
  • Jobs data and economic growth
  • Bond yield movement
  • Risk-on or risk-off market mood
  • Geopolitical stress and safe-haven demand

Major currency themes

The U.S. dollar often responds to Fed policy, Treasury yields, inflation expectations, and global risk appetite. EURUSD may react strongly to European Central Bank expectations and U.S. dollar movement. USDJPY is often sensitive to yield spreads and Bank of Japan policy expectations.

Commodity-linked currencies such as AUD, CAD, and NZD may react to commodities, China demand, global growth, and risk sentiment.

How to use forex sentiment

Public forex sentiment can help show what currency themes traders are watching. However, it should be compared with technical trend, rate expectations, and upcoming economic calendar risk.
