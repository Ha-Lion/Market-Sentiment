Forex Guide

Forex Sentiment

Forex sentiment is the public mood around currencies, central banks, inflation, interest rates, jobs data, growth expectations, and geopolitical risk.

Public Sentiment Dash helps organize FX-related headlines so users can quickly see whether currency narratives are leaning bullish, bearish, mixed, or neutral.

U.S. Dollar

Tracks public narrative around DXY, Treasury yields, Fed policy, inflation, jobs, and dollar strength or weakness.

Major Pairs

Covers major FX pairs such as EUR/USD, GBP/USD, USD/JPY, USD/CAD, AUD/USD, NZD/USD, and USD/CHF.

Macro Drivers

Highlights public coverage around central banks, inflation, yields, recession risk, and economic data.

What moves forex sentiment?

Forex sentiment often reacts to interest-rate expectations, inflation surprises, central bank speeches, economic data, risk appetite, trade policy, and geopolitical tension.

A currency can strengthen when public narrative favors higher rates, stronger growth, safe-haven demand, or better economic expectations. It can weaken when coverage focuses on lower rates, weak data, political risk, or worsening economic momentum.
