Market Sentiment Guide

Market sentiment is the public mood surrounding financial markets. It can come from news headlines, investor commentary, analyst expectations, social attention, macro events, and price behavior.

Public Sentiment Dash helps organize that noisy public narrative into a clearer view of what markets are focused on and whether the tone is leaning bullish, bearish, mixed, or neutral.

Why Sentiment Matters

Sentiment can influence short-term market behavior because traders often react to fear, greed, uncertainty, expectations, and breaking news.

However, sentiment alone is not enough. Strong sentiment can reverse quickly when price action, economic data, earnings, liquidity, or central bank policy changes.

How to Use This Site

  • Start with the global public sentiment score on the home page.
  • Use the interactive dashboard to filter by market or instrument.
  • Check source votes to see where tone is coming from.
  • Read relevant headlines before making any market interpretation.
  • Confirm everything with price action, technicals, fundamentals, and risk management.

Important Warning

Market sentiment is not a trading signal. It is an information layer. A bullish public narrative does not guarantee price will rise, and a bearish public narrative does not guarantee price will fall.
