Market Sentiment Guide

Market sentiment is the public mood surrounding financial markets. It can come from news headlines, investor commentary, analyst expectations, social discussion, and the way financial media frames current events.

What is market sentiment?

Market sentiment is not the same as value. It is the emotional and narrative layer of the market. It reflects whether the public conversation sounds optimistic, fearful, cautious, excited, defensive, or uncertain.

Sentiment can move quickly because headlines and narratives spread faster than full fundamental analysis.

Why sentiment matters

  • It can affect short-term volatility.
  • It can reveal what markets are paying attention to.
  • It can show whether fear or optimism is dominating the conversation.
  • It can help identify crowded narratives.
  • It can warn when market mood is shifting.

Sentiment vs fundamentals vs technicals

Sentiment

What people and financial media are saying.

Fundamentals

What the economic and financial reality suggests.

Technicals

What price behavior and trend structure are showing.
