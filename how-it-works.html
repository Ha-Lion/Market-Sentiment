System overview

How Public Sentiment Dash Works

Public Sentiment Dash collects public market headlines, organizes them by market and instrument, scores the public tone, and presents the result in a cleaner dashboard.

It is designed to help users quickly see what public financial sources are emphasizing before doing deeper technical, fundamental, and risk analysis.

The Process

1

Public Sources Are Scanned

The engine checks configured public financial sources and collects headlines related to indexes, bonds, forex, crypto, commodities, and macro themes.

2

Headlines Are Classified

Headlines are matched to markets and instruments using relevance logic. This helps separate broad market news from specific instrument-related headlines.

3

Public Sentiment Is Scored

Source votes and headline tone are converted into bullish, bearish, mixed, or neutral readings. A 0–100 score gives a fast public sentiment snapshot.

4

Market Focus Is Built

The dashboard identifies which market groups and instruments appear most often in the latest scan, helping users see where public attention is concentrated.

5

Interactive Pages Present the Data

The home page gives the broad view. The interactive dashboard lets users filter by market and instrument. The News & Articles page lets users search the published feed.

Public Sentiment vs Technicals

Public sentiment reflects the tone and attention in public financial coverage.

Technical direction is different. It reflects price-based trend readings when available, so the site shows Daily, Weekly, and Monthly direction instead of turning it into a confusing fake score.

Why Scores Can Change

Scores can change as new headlines appear, sources update, market focus shifts, or the balance of bullish and bearish coverage changes.

A high score does not guarantee price will rise. A low score does not guarantee price will fall. It reflects public narrative from the available scan.

Important Limitations

Rule-Based Relevance
Some headlines can still be misclassified when keywords overlap. AI classification can improve this later.
Not Trading Advice
The dashboard is informational and educational. It is not a trade signal or financial recommendation.
Source Availability
Public sources may change, block, delay, or alter feeds. Status dots and trust data help show scan health.
Market Confirmation Needed
Always confirm with price action, fundamentals, technicals, economic data, and risk management.
